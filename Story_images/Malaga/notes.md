## Raw Notes

The cruise ship docked right in the heart of Málaga — we stepped off and were immediately in the city. A big park and wide walkway, people going to work, the cruise terminal woven into everyday life rather than sealed off from it. We walked slowly toward the Alcazaba fort, soaking in the sun and the beauty of everything — the people, the architecture, all immaculate.

After an hour at the Alcazaba we came down around 11:30 and headed toward the Picasso Museum. We stopped outside for tapas — the best ensalada rusa I have ever had. While waiting in the queue we met a couple from our own ship who had no tickets and were about to be turned away. We helped them get the last two tickets for the 2 PM slot. A small kindness that felt in keeping with the day.

The Picasso Museum is extraordinary. Smaller than Paris or New York, yet packed with every phase of his life — classical draughtsmanship, the Blue Period, Cubism, late work — without being overwhelming. Because the tickets are limited it is never crowded. This is how a museum should be experienced. Those who are into the classical-to-modern arc must see this. It is not going on and on; it is precise.

Then the cathedral — enormous, bone-crushing in its impressiveness. A little overwhelming after everything else, but undeniable.
