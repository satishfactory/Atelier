## Raw Notes

Today I finally got to visit Matisse's Morocco — this was my dream that came true after so many years of waiting. Our tour guide agreed to take us to the Grand Hotel Villa de France, and we found our way through the hotel into Room 35, where Matisse painted some of his most iconic Moroccan works. When the assistant — dressed in the French-Moroccan hotel uniform — opened the window, I looked out at the exact view Matisse had painted. The experience cannot be forgotten. Time froze. That painting had captured the essence of how Morocco looked almost 100 years ago, and here it was, unchanged.

Then we also visited the iconic grill patterns around the hotel — the same ones that appear in his various paintings. After that, our guide took us through the alleys to the famous Kasbah gate, where red light pours through the door. Incredibly, a couple of people were sitting there in exactly the same spot as the figure in his painting. Then we stumbled upon the mosque — freshly painted that morning — stunningly beautiful. Our guide told us: that is where Matisse sat and painted. It is possible to literally follow his footsteps, even after 100 years, to the exact spots. The vibrancy of the light and colours is hard to imagine without experiencing it.

Ibn Battuta's resting place — the first travel blogger in the world, 700 years before Instagram.

The snake charmer modelled for me for a fee. He was ready and very photogenic. He will appear in a new painting.
